基本信息

标题：What Percentage of the Human Brain Is Used?

副标题：Debunking the 10% Myth

By Alane Lim | Updated on August 04, 2019

原地址：https://www.thoughtco.com/percentage-of-human-brain-used-4159438

文章结构

（Chat-GPT 3.5辅助）

Introduction
The idea that humans only use 10 percent of their brain power is a complete myth.
It is deeply entrenched in the collective consciousness, influencing even some cinematic productions.

Field1: Neuropsychology
Rebuttal1. With the aid of brain imaging techniques, scientists have substantiated that every component of the brain is utilized in our daily activities.
Rebuttal2. People who have undergone brain damage may face an impact on their daily functioning. ( If our brain utilization is limited to only 10 percent, then any damage to the brain would have minimal negative impact on us. )

Field2: Evolution
Over millions of years of natural selection, the contemporary adult human brain exhibits a significantly higher energy consumption relative to that of other animals.

The Origin of the Myth
Source1: Self-improvement books
Source2: “Silent” brain areas from older neuroscience research

结构和逻辑非常清晰，总分的结构方式，首先引出讨论的内容“大脑使用量10%”这个广泛传播的谣言，并分两个方面进行说明：第一个是对谣言的科学反驳，第二个是溯源谣言的产生。反驳里还分脑神经和进化两个领域。

psychic
[Gk psychikos of the soul, fr. psychē soul]

telekinesis
the production of motion in objects (as by a spiritualistic medium) without contact or other physical means
【派】telekinetic, telekinetically

debunk
【释义】vt. 揭穿…的真面目: to reveal the true nature of
【例】debunk a supposed miracle drug 揭穿所谓的灵丹妙药的真面目
【近】uncloak, uncover, undress, unmask, show up
【反】camouflage, cloak, disguise, mask 继续伪装，掩盖

protagonist
【词根词缀】Prot- (原始的、最初的) + agon (奋斗、努力) + -ist
【释义】 the principal character in a literary work (as a drama or story)
【近】 principal character, leading actor
（GRE无）

trope
[L tropus, fr. Gk tropos turn, way, manner, style, trope, fr. trepein to turn]
a word or expression used in a ﬁgurative sense
文章中应该就是“表达”的意思

integral
【释义】adj. 完整的: not lacking any part or member that properly belongs to it
【例】the belief that athletics are essential to an integral life 坚信运动是完整的人生所不可或缺的一部分
【近】comprehensive, entire, full, grand, intact, perfect, plenary, total, whole
【反】imperfect, incomplete 不完美的，不完整的；partial 部分的

resonance
n. 洪亮（由resonant派生的名词）
【释义】adj. (声音)洪亮的，共鸣的： strong and deep in tone
【例】A deep resonant voice rang out.传来了洪亮的声音。
【近】consonant, orotund, plangent, resounding, reverberant, sonorous, vibrant
【反】faint, low, muffled, muted, smothered, soft, weak 声音模糊的，低声的

splotch
[perh. blend of spot and blotch]
【派】splotchy
【近】spot, blotch

allure
【考法】vt. 吸引: to attract or delight as if by magic
【例】an alluring smile 迷人的微笑
【派】alluring adj. 诱惑的，有吸引力的
【近】entice, seduce, solicit, tempt, captivate, enchant, lead on
【反】unattractive 无吸引力的

latent
【考法】adj. 潜在的，不活跃的：present or potentialt but not evidentt or active
【例】a latent infection 潜伏性传染病 | He has a latent talent for acting that he hasn’t had a chance to express yet.他有着潜在的表演的天赋，只不过他还没有机会来表现这一才能。
【派】latency n. 潜伏期
【近】dormant, fallow, inert, inoperative
【反】apparent, evident, manifest, obvious, plain 明显的; active 活跃的
